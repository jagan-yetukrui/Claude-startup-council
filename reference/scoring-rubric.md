# Scoring rubric — the five axes, 1 to 5

The definitive per-axis anchor set. Every member's private scorecard and the judge's final scorecard
resolve each axis to an integer 1–5 using these anchors, always in fixed order: **Pain →
Willingness-to-Pay → Wedge → Distribution → Timing/Moat.** The 25-point total maps to the verdict
band: **≥20 BUILD · 13–19 PIVOT · ≤12 KILL.** Read the global law first — it overrides any single-axis
anchor where they seem to disagree.

## Global scoring law (applies to every axis)

- **1–2 = the research is empty or points the wrong way.** A 1 means the dossier gives you nothing
  for this axis, or the closest evidence actively contradicts the claim. A 2 means only fragile
  E0–E1 support exists AND there is a real counter-signal.
- **3 = plausible but unproven.** The claim is reasonable, but its best support is E0–E2 (assertion,
  survey, stated intent, market-logic analogy). A 3 is the honest score for "could be true, not
  shown." **An axis whose best available support is E1 or lower may NOT exceed 3** — no exceptions.
- **4–5 REQUIRE a specific E3+ dossier fact, quoted inline in that scorecard row.** A 4 rests on one
  solid E3/E4 fact. A 5 rests on multiple independent E3/E4 facts that converge. If you cannot quote
  the observed-behavior or money-changes-hands fact in the row, the score is capped at 3.
- **At most TWO axes may score exactly 3.** This forces commitment: a scorecard of all 3s is a
  refusal to judge, not a judgment. If three or more axes want a 3, break the ties with evidence —
  the weaker ones fall to 2, the better-supported ones rise to 4 (only if an E3+ fact exists).
- **Every 3 must state its move-condition** in the row: the specific evidence that would push it to
  a 4 (found) or to a 2 (its absence confirmed). "Pain 3 — E2 survey only; a live complaint thread
  moves this to 4, a silent market confirmed moves it to 2."
- **Founder-revenue absence is NEVER grounds for a low score.** The founder is pre-launch; having no
  sales, LOIs, prepays, or pilots is the normal state and is not evidence. Score down only when
  *market* research is absent or contradicting. Keep "no founder proof" and "no market proof"
  strictly separate — only the second lowers a score.
- **Score up when the evidence is there — don't deflate to look tough.** When a real E3+ market fact
  supports an axis, give it the 4 or 5 it earned. Reflexively marking a well-supported axis down "to
  be safe" is the cynicism failure and is as wrong as inflating it. A genuinely strong idea should
  produce a genuinely high total; the anchors are symmetric, not a ceiling that keeps everything low.

## 1. Pain

**Definition:** how acute is the problem in the real world — an urgent painkiller people already
route around, or a nice-to-have vitamin nobody loses sleep over.

**E3+ at pre-launch looks like:** dated forum/Reddit/review complaints in the sufferer's own words;
documented workarounds people build to cope (spreadsheets, Zapier hacks, manual labor); churn/rant
posts about existing tools; support-ticket or G2/Trustpilot volume on the pain. E4 = people already
paying to make this specific pain go away.

| Score | Meaning | Evidence strength |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| 1 | No trace of the pain anywhere, or research shows people are content with the status quo | E3-absence with counter-signal, or contradicting E3 |
| 2 | Only the founder/analogy asserts the pain; a silent market or a "meh" counter-signal exists | E0–E1, plus a real negative |
| 3 | Pain is plausible and someone states it, but only via survey/intent or market logic | best support E2 or below → capped here |
| 4 | At least one dated, specific complaint or documented workaround in the sufferer's own words | one quoted E3 fact |
| 5 | Multiple independent complaint threads / workarounds / paid pain-relief, converging on acuteness | multiple E3–E4 facts |

**GOOD:** `Pain 4 — E3: r/freelance thread (2026-03), 40+ upvotes, "I rebuild the same invoice
spreadsheet every month and still miss late payers."` The score points at an observed, dated,
in-their-words complaint.
**WEAK:** `Pain 4 — the founder has felt this deeply for years.` Founder conviction is E0; it caps at
2–3 and can never justify a 4. Rewrite as a 3 with a move-condition, or find the E3 thread.

## 2. Willingness-to-Pay

**Definition:** does the *market* already pay for comparable solutions — is there an existing budget
category with money moving in it that this idea plausibly taps.

**Explicitly market-based. FORBIDDEN as the basis for any WTP score:** founder revenue, the founder's
own sales, signed LOIs, prepayments, pilot invoices, or waitlist "I'd pay" promises. Those are
either founder-traction (irrelevant at this stage) or E2 stated intent. WTP is scored from what
*other* companies charge and *demonstrably get paid*.

**E3+ at pre-launch looks like:** a comparable/competitor with real paying customers; public live
pricing that people demonstrably buy (reviews mentioning the price, renewal/seat counts); a named,
funded budget line the buyer already spends in ("teams already pay 200 dollars a month for X").

| Score | Meaning | Evidence strength |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| 1 | No one pays for anything like this; adjacent tools are free/abandoned, or buyers refuse to pay | contradicting E3/E4 |
| 2 | Only intent ("people say they'd pay") or a strained analogy; no live paid comparable found | E1–E2 |
| 3 | A budget category plausibly exists by market logic, but no comparable's real pricing is cited | best support E1–E2 → capped here |
| 4 | One named comparable with live pricing people demonstrably pay, quoted in the row | one quoted E4 fact |
| 5 | Several comparables charging and getting paid, or a large observable budget category with proof | multiple E4 facts |

**GOOD:** `WTP 4 — E4: comparable "LateFee" lists live pricing at 29 dollars a month and shows a
public wall of 300+ paying agencies.` Money observably changes hands in the market.
**WEAK:** `WTP 4 — three waitlist signups said they'd pay 20 dollars a month.` Stated intent is E2 and
here it is founder-side; it may not exceed 3 and cannot ground a 4. If it were founder *revenue*, it
would be barred entirely — never a factor.

## 3. Wedge

**Definition:** is there a winnable first beachhead — a narrow, specific slice this can actually own
before fighting the whole market.

**E3+ at pre-launch looks like:** an identifiable, reachable sub-segment already behaving as a group
(a named community, a niche where incumbents visibly under-serve, documented unmet need in that
slice); evidence that this narrow group churns from or hacks around the generalist tools.

| Score | Meaning | Evidence strength |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| 1 | No defensible slice; idea is "everyone" or collides head-on with an entrenched incumbent | contradicting E3 |
| 2 | A slice is named but arbitrary, with no sign the group behaves distinctly or is under-served | E0–E1 |
| 3 | A plausible beachhead is described by market logic, but no observed under-served behavior cited | best support E1–E2 → capped here |
| 4 | One concrete under-served segment with observed evidence they lack a fit tool, quoted in-row | one quoted E3 fact |
| 5 | A sharp beachhead plus observed pull (that group already hacking/asking/switching), converging | multiple E3 facts |

**GOOD:** `Wedge 4 — E3: 3 dance-studio owners on a Facebook group (2026-02) say scheduling apps
"assume a gym, not recurring class packs" and export to Excel instead.` A named, observed,
under-served slice.
**WEAK:** `Wedge 4 — we'll start with small businesses and expand.` "Small businesses" is not a wedge
and there is no evidence; caps at 2.

## 4. Distribution

**Definition:** is there a believable, affordable way to reach these customers repeatably — not one
lucky launch, but a channel that keeps working.

**E3+ at pre-launch looks like:** comparables visibly acquiring through a specific channel (SEO
ranking on the pain keywords, an active community/subreddit the buyers gather in, a partner/
integration marketplace with traffic); observable proof the audience congregates somewhere
reachable. E4 = a comparable openly attributing paid growth to a channel with unit economics.

| Score | Meaning | Evidence strength |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| 1 | Buyers are diffuse and unreachable; no channel found, or the obvious channel is saturated/closed | contradicting E3 |
| 2 | A channel is asserted ("we'll do content / go viral") with no evidence it reaches this buyer | E0–E1 |
| 3 | A channel is plausible by analogy to similar products, but no observed reach for THIS buyer cited | best support E1–E2 → capped here |
| 4 | One concrete channel with observed evidence the buyers are reachable there, quoted in-row | one quoted E3 fact |
| 5 | A proven, affordable channel (comparable demonstrably acquiring there) with converging signals | multiple E3–E4 facts |

**GOOD:** `Dist 4 — E3: the target buyers cluster in r/msp (180k members) where two comparables run
weekly AMAs and top-rank on the pain query.` An observed, reachable congregation point.
**WEAK:** `Dist 4 — TikTok has huge reach so we'll go viral.` Generic reach is not evidence this buyer
is reachable there; E0, caps at 2.

## 5. Timing/Moat

**Definition:** why now (a real enabler that just changed) and does any lead hold once incumbents and
copycats notice — durability, not just a head start.

**E3+ at pre-launch looks like:** a dated, named enabler (a regulation, a platform/API opening, a
cost curve crossing, a behavior shift) with observed downstream effects; a graveyard of prior
attempts whose deaths you can read the *cause* of; a structural moat (data network effect, switching
cost, integration lock-in) evidenced in a comparable.

| Score | Meaning | Evidence strength |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| 1 | Idea has been tried and died repeatedly for the same reason, and nothing has changed | contradicting E3/E4 (graveyard) |
| 2 | No credible "why now," OR 2+ graveyard deaths share a structural cause and no new enabler named | E0–E1, or the graveyard cap below |
| 3 | A "why now" is argued by market logic, but the enabler is unproven and no moat is evidenced | best support E1–E2 → capped here |
| 4 | One dated, named enabler with observed effect, OR one evidenced structural moat, quoted in-row | one quoted E3 fact |
| 5 | A real dated enabler AND an evidenced durable moat, converging from independent facts | multiple E3–E4 facts |

**GRAVEYARD CAP (hard rule):** if the dossier shows **2 or more prior deaths that share a structural
cause** (e.g. "consumers won't pay for personal-finance apps") and **no credible NEW enabler** is
named that dissolves that cause, Timing/Moat is **capped at 2** regardless of other signals. A new
enabler must be specific and dated, not "but AI now" hand-waving.

**GOOD:** `Timing 4 — E3: Stripe's 2026 tax API (GA'd Jan) removed the compliance build that killed
"TaxBot" and two peers per their shutdown post-mortems.` A dated enabler tied to the graveyard cause.
**WEAK:** `Timing 4 — now is the perfect time because of AI.` Undated, unspecific, E0; caps at 2 (and
if a shared-cause graveyard exists, the cap binds).

## Scoring checklist (run before finalizing the scorecard)

Before you write the total, confirm every line:

1. **Order.** Axes appear as Pain → WTP → Wedge → Distribution → Timing/Moat.
2. **Every 4 or 5 quotes an E3+ fact inline.** No 4/5 rests on E0–E2. If the quote is missing, drop
   the score to 3.
3. **No axis above 3 whose best support is E1 or lower.** Re-check each — analogy/TAM-logic caps at 3.
4. **No more than two 3s.** If a third 3 remains, break it with evidence to a 2 or a 4.
5. **Every 3 names its move-condition** — the specific evidence that makes it a 4, and what confirms
   it a 2.
6. **WTP is market-based.** Its score cites comparable pricing / a real budget category — never
   founder revenue, sales, LOIs, or prepays.
7. **Timing/Moat graveyard cap honored** — 2+ shared-cause deaths with no dated new enabler ⇒ ≤2.
8. **No axis penalized for founder-revenue absence.** Confirm every low score points at absent or
   contradicting *market* research, not at the founder having no sales.
9. **Dollar amounts spelled out** — never a dollar sign immediately followed by a digit anywhere in
   the scorecard.
10. **Total and band.** Sum the five integers; map to ≥20 BUILD · 13–19 PIVOT · ≤12 KILL. The judge
    may only move the verdict downward from the band, never upward.
